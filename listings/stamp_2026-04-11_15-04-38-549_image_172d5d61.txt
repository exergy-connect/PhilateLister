### **Catalog Identification**
**Sweden Scott #24** (Facit #24). 
*Justification:* The stamp is a 30-öre "Ringtyp" (Ring Type) in brown. The issue comes in two perforation gauges: Perf 14 (issued 1872, Scott #24) and Perf 13 (issued 1877, Scott #35). The clear postmark date of 1872 definitively identifies this as the earlier Perf 14 issue.

### **Condition Report**
*   **Overall Condition:** Used, Good to Fine.
*   **Centering:** The design is shifted somewhat to the bottom, touching or nearly touching the bottom perforations. 
*   **Color/Impression:** The underlying brownish/lilac-brown color is still clearly visible beneath the heavy postmark. No obvious thins, creases, or tears are visible from the front view.
*   **Cancel Effect:** The cancel is very heavy and bold, completely obscuring the central numeral, though it makes for an excellent town and date strike.

### **Perforation Details**
*   **Gauge:** Standard Perf 14 for this issue. 
*   **Dent Count (approximate):** 
    *   Top (Horizontal): 14 dents
    *   Bottom (Horizontal): 14 dents
    *   Left (Vertical): 15 dents
    *   Right (Vertical): 14 dents
*   **Flaws:** The perforations are generally sound for the era. There is a slightly blunted/shortened perforation at the top left corner and minor roughness on the bottom left corner. 

### **Cancel Details**
*   **Format:** Classic Swedish circular date stamp. 
*   **Town/City:** **TRAHERYD** (Traryd, located in Småland). The town name is struck very clearly in an arc across the top.
*   **Date:** **November 20, 1872**. 
    *   *Reading the Swedish Cancel:* The large central number **"20"** indicates the day of the month. The bottom line reads **"18 11 72"**, which represents the century (18), the month (11 = November), and the year (72).
*   **Day of the Week:** November 20, 1872, was a **Wednesday**.
