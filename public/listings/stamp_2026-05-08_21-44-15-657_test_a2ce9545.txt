This used bicolored stamp from Denmark is a classic example of the 19th-century "Bifavor" (Bicolored) issues.

### **eBay Listing Title**
**Denmark Scott #29 Used - 8 Øre Bicolored Gray & Carmine - Copenhagen Numeral 1 Cancel**

### **Identification & Catalog Number**
*   **Country:** Denmark
*   **Issue:** Bicolored Numeral Issue
*   **Value:** 8 Øre
*   **Colors:** Gray center and carmine frame
*   **Catalog Number:** Likely **Scott #29** (issued 1875–1879, perf 14 x 13.5). A later printing (Scott #43) with a different perforation gauge (12.75) exists, but the appearance is consistent with the earlier classic series.

### **Condition Report**
*   **Overall Grade:** Very Fine (VF) for the issue.
*   **Centering:** Exceptionally well-centered for a bicolored stamp, with even margins on all sides.
*   **Perforations:** The perforations appear intact and complete. A manual count from the image shows approximately **13 horizontal holes** and **14 vertical holes**, which aligns with the standard 14 x 13.5 gauge.
*   **Surface:** No visible creases, thins, or tears are apparent from the face. The colors remain distinct and have not significantly bled or faded.

### **Cancellation Details**
*   **Type:** Numeral Cancel "1" within three concentric rings.
*   **Location:** **Copenhagen (København)**. Numeral "1" was assigned to the capital city, the most significant postal hub in Denmark.
*   **Clarity:** The cancel is bold and well-struck, centered slightly to the right, leaving much of the "8 ØRE" denomination and "DANMARK" country name legible.

### **Historical Context: Copenhagen**
At the time this stamp was in circulation (circa 1875), Copenhagen was undergoing a period of rapid industrialization and expansion. Its population was approximately 200,000, and it served as the undisputed center of Danish commerce, culture, and government. As the primary port and railway terminus, the "1" numeral cancel is the most common but also signifies the heart of the Danish postal system during the Golden Age of philately.
