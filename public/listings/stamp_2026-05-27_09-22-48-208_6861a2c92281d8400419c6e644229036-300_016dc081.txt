This is a used specimen of the **Netherlands 1872 Coat of Arms issue**, specifically the **15 cent orange** (Scott #22).

### eBay Listing Details

**Title:** Netherlands 1872 15c Orange (Scott 22) - Clear DOESBURG 1881 Town Cancel
**Scott Number:** Netherlands #22 (15c Orange)

**Condition Report:**
*   **Overall:** Used condition with a very crisp, legible cancellation.
*   **Centering:** Off-center to the bottom and right, with the design nearly touching the perforations at the bottom.
*   **Color:** The original orange color has deepened/oxidized into a brownish-orange shade, which is common for this 19th-century issue.
*   **Perforations:** Generally intact; the count is approximately 12.5 horizontal by 12 vertical (standard for one of the common perforation varieties of this issue). Some minor blunting is visible on the right-hand teeth.
*   **Faults:** No visible tears, thins, or creases from the face.

**Cancel Information:**
*   **Type:** Circular Date Stamp (CDS).
*   **City:** **Doesburg**, a historic Hanseatic city in the province of Gelderland. At the time of this cancel (c. 1880), Doesburg was a fortified town with a population of approximately 4,700. It served as an important regional hub at the confluence of the Oude IJssel and IJssel rivers.
*   **Date:** **15 11 81** (November 15, 1881).
*   **Weekday:** November 15, 1881, was a **Tuesday**, a standard day for postal operations.

For your consideration is a nice used example of the Netherlands 1872 15-cent orange Coat of Arms stamp (Scott #22). This specimen is distinguished by a remarkably clear and well-struck "DOESBURG" circular date stamp dated November 15, 1881.
