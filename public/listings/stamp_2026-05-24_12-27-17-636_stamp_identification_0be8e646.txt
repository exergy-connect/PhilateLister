### eBay Title
**US Scott #63 1c Blue Benjamin Franklin (1861 Issue) - Two Unused Examples**

### Catalog Identification
*   **Scott Number:** **#63** (1861 1¢ Blue, Benjamin Franklin). 
*   **Identification Note:** This design features Benjamin Franklin in a circular frame with "U.S. POSTAGE" at the top and "ONE CENT" at the bottom. Without a scan of the back to check for "grills" (impressed patterns used to prevent reuse), these are identified as the standard #63 issue. If a grill is present, they would be Scott #92, #93, or #102.

### Condition Report
*   **Centering:** 
    *   **Left Copy:** Features Fine-Very Fine (F-VF) centering. The design is well-positioned, though slightly shifted toward the top.
    *   **Right Copy:** Poor centering. The design is shifted significantly downward, nearly touching the bottom perforations.
*   **Color:** The left example shows a deep, rich blue typical of the 1861 printing. The right example appears slightly paler or perhaps more aged.
*   **General:** Both stamps appear to be unused with no visible postal cancellations. Neither shows signs of major tears or creases from the front, though the right copy has a slightly uneven perforation line at the bottom.

### Perforation Details
*   **Count:** These are **Perf 12** stamps, which was the standard for the 1861 series.
*   **Observation:**
    *   **Horizontal:** Approximately 11–12 perforation tips across the top and bottom.
    *   **Vertical:** Approximately 15–16 perforation tips along the sides.
    *   **Condition:** On the left stamp, the perforations are mostly intact and well-defined. On the right stamp, several perforations at the bottom and right side appear slightly shorter or "nibbed," likely due to rough separation when originally removed from the sheet.

### Cancellation
*   **Details:** There is **no postal cancellation** visible on either stamp. They appear to be in "Unused" condition. Given their age and lack of cancels, they are likely "No Gum" (NG) or "Original Gum" (OG), but this can only be confirmed by inspecting the reverse.

### Listing Body Text

Both stamps appear unused with no cancels. They are perfect for a collector looking to fill a space for this early US classic or for a specialist interested in shades and centering varieties of the 1861 issue.
