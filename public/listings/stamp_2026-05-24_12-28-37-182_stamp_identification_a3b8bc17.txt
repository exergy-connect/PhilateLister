This listing information is prepared for two specimens of the United States 1c Benjamin Franklin "Bank Note" issue, which were produced by two different printing companies in the early 1870s.

### **eBay Title**
**USA 1870-73 1c Franklin Blue - Scott #145 or #156 - Unused Pair, Bank Note Issue**

### **Identification & Catalog Numbers**
*   **Scott Number:** **145** (National Bank Note Co., 1870) or **156** (Continental Bank Note Co., 1873).
*   **Justification:** These stamps represent the 1c Franklin design from the famous "Bank Note" era. 
    *   **Scott 145** is the National Bank Note Company printing, often found in a bright, almost violet-blue "ultramarine" shade. 
    *   **Scott 156** is the Continental Bank Note Company printing, which features a "secret mark"—a small dash or heavy shading in the pearl (ball) immediately to the left of the word "ONE" at the bottom.
    *   The stamp on the left displays the saturated, bright blue shade frequently associated with Scott 145. Without a microscopic look at the secret mark area, they are listed as either 145 or 156 per the dealer's notes.

### **Condition Report**

*   **Left Stamp:** Strong color and excellent eye appeal. Centering is Fine-Very Fine (F-VF), shifted slightly toward the bottom. 
*   **Right Stamp:** Duller or perhaps faded blue shade. Centering is Fine (F), shifted significantly to the right. 
*   **Gum:** Appearance suggests unused/no gum or disturbed gum; physical inspection is required to confirm if original gum (OG) is present.
