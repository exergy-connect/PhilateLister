### Identification: Scott #O6
This stamp is **Scott #O6**, the 12 öre blue from the first Official issue of Sweden. It is distinguished from the later 1877 issue (Scott #O16) by its **Perf 14** gauge, which is consistent with the count of 14 horizontal and 14-15 vertical perforation tips seen on this example.

### Condition Report
*   **Grade:** Used.
*   **Centering:** Shifted slightly to the left, which is common for this 19th-century issue.
*   **Perforations:** Perf 14. Generally intact, though showing some typical 1870s roughness, particularly along the left margin which appears slightly irregular from separation.
*   **Faults:** The stamp shows light overall toning and some minor soiling/ink residue consistent with postal use and its age of nearly 150 years. No major thins or tears are visible from the face.

### Cancellation Details
*   **City:** **V. ÅSBO** (likely Västra Åsbo). Västra Åsbo was a district (härad) in the Scania (Skåne) region of Sweden. 
*   **Date:** The circular date stamp (CDS) is clearly dated **18 11 78** (November 18, 1878). 
*   **Historical Context:** November 18, 1878, was a **Monday**. At the time of this cancellation, the Åsbo region was primarily an agricultural hub. The parish of Åsbo in nearby Östergötland had a population of approximately 2,700 people in 1880, while the larger Västra Åsbo district in Skåne was a significant administrative and rural center for the southern region.
