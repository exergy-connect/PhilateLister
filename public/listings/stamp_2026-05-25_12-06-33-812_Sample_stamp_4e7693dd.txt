**Cancel Detail:** Features a very clear and strikes-bold "ÅSBO" circular date stamp.
*   **Date:** November 12, 1878.
*   **Format:** Following the Swedish postal convention of the era, the central digit "12" represents the day. The bottom row "18 11 78" indicates the year (18...78) split around the month (11).
*   **Weekday:** November 12, 1878, was a **Tuesday**, a standard business day for the Swedish postal service.
**City/Region Information:** Åsbo is a rural parish located in Boxholm Municipality, Östergötland County. In the late 1870s, Åsbo was a small agricultural community with a population of approximately 2,000 people. It served as a local hub for the surrounding farming region, making clear strikes from this period relatively desirable for regional postal history collectors.

This particular stamp is notable for its exceptionally crisp and legible ÅSBO cancel dated November 12, 1878. While the centering is poor and the left perforations show some damage, the strike is excellent for a town collector or a student of 19th-century Swedish postal markings.
