This specimen is a **Denmark Scott #31**, the 8 øre grey and carmine bicolored issue first released in 1875. 

### Identification & Chronology
*   **Catalog Number:** Scott #31 (8 øre).
*   **Cancel:** Very Fine (VF) strike of numeral **152** within concentric rings. 
*   **Postal History:** Numeral 152 was originally assigned to Flensborg. Following the territorial changes of 1864, it was reassigned to the town of **Skibby** on the island of Sjælland. This assignment was active from 1864 through 1884, which aligns perfectly with the 1875 issue date of this stamp.
*   **Varieties:** Uploader notes identify this as **Print 4** with an **inverted watermark**. While the watermark is not visible from the front, Scott #31 is known for multiple printings and watermark orientations.

### Condition Report
*   **Centering:** Very Fine (VF); the design is well-centered within the perforations, which is desirable for this often poorly-centered bicolored issue.
*   **Perforations:** Generally sound and intact. One or two perforation tips at the top left appear slightly irregular but do not constitute significant faults.
*   **Color:** Bright and fresh; the carmine frame shows no significant fading.
*   **Cancel Quality:** Bold, centered, and fully legible strike, making it a "premium" cancel example for collectors of Danish numeral cancels.
