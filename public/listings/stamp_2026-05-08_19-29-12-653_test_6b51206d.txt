This is an example of the popular **Denmark bi-color issue**, specifically the **8 øre** value from the series first introduced in **1875**.

### **Identification**
*   **Country:** Denmark
*   **Issue Date:** 1875–1903 (Bi-color series)
*   **Catalog Number:** Likely **Scott #28** (8ø Slate & Carmine). The presence of a numeral cancel strongly favors the earlier perf 14 x 13.5 issue (Scott 28) over the later 1895 re-issue (Scott 43, perf 12.75), as numeral cancels were largely phased out in Denmark starting in 1884.
*   **Denomination:** 8 Øre
*   **Color:** Grey/Slate center with a Carmine/Red frame.

### **Cancel Analysis**
*   **Type:** Three-ring numeral cancel (standard Danish format for the era).
*   **Numeral:** The digits appear to be **"21"**. 
    *   The first digit is clearly a "2" with its characteristic top curve and horizontal base.
    *   The second digit is a vertical bar, consistent with a Danish numeral "1".
*   **Town Assignment:** In the Danish postal system of this period, **numeral 21** was assigned to **Assens**, a town on the island of Funen.
*   **Chronology:** The 1875 issue date of the stamp aligns perfectly with the active use of numeral cancels in Denmark (1851–1884).

### **Condition Report**
*   **Perforations:** Generally sound. There is some minor irregularity/shortening of teeth on the left edge, but most teeth are present and well-defined.
*   **Centering:** The stamp is reasonably well-centered for this issue, slightly shifted towards the top right.
*   **Color/Impression:** The bi-color printing shows good registration and fresh colors. 
*   **Faults:** No obvious thins, tears, or creases are visible from the face. The cancel strike is heavy and bold.
