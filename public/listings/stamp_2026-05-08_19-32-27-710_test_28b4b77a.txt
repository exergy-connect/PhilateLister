This stamp is a Denmark 12 øre bicolored issue from the late 19th century, featuring the clear and well-centered 3-ring numeral cancel **152**.

### Identification & Chronology
*   **Country:** Denmark
*   **Issue:** Bicolored Coat of Arms (Arms of Denmark in oval)
*   **Denomination:** 12 øre
*   **Color:** Carmine and grey/blue-grey
*   **Catalog Number:** Likely **Scott #29** (issued 1875, perf 14 x 13½) or **Scott #42** (issued 1882, perf 12½). Definitive identification between these two varieties requires a physical perforation gauge.
*   **Cancel:** 3-ring numeral cancel **152**.
*   **Postal History:** Numeral 152 was originally assigned to Flensborg, but following the loss of that territory in 1864, it was reassigned to **Skibby** (on the island of Zealand). This cancel was used in Skibby from October 1, 1864, until it was retired at the end of 1884.
*   **Consistency Check:** The cancel date window (ending 1884) is perfectly consistent with the 12 øre bicolored issue, which entered circulation in 1875.

### Condition Report
*   **Perforations:** Generally complete on all sides. The perforations appear consistent with the typical rougher separation of the period.
*   **Centering:** Above average for this issue; the design is shifted slightly to the left but remains well-framed.
*   **Cancel Quality:** Excellent strike. The numeral "152" is fully legible and centrally placed on the stamp.
*   **Faults:** No major thin spots or tears are visible from the scan. The stamp shows a sound appearance for a used example of this era.
