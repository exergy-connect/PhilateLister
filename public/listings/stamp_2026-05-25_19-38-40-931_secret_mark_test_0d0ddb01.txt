This pair of stamps provides an excellent side-by-side study of the **United States 1870-1873 Bank Note Issues**, specifically highlighting the diagnostic "secret mark" used to distinguish different printing companies.

### **eBay Listing Title**
**US Scott #145 & #156 1c Blue Franklin Bank Note Pair - "Secret Mark" Study - XF/VF**

### **Identification & Catalog Numbers**
*   **Left Stamp: Scott #145 (National Bank Note Co., 1870).** This is the original printing. It is identified by the absence of the "secret mark" in the scrollwork at the bottom.
*   **Right Stamp: Scott #156 (Continental Bank Note Co., 1873).** This printing includes the **secret mark**: a small, dark crescent-shaped dash added inside the ornamental "ball" located to the left of the word "ONE" in the bottom scroll. 
*   *Note: While Scott #182 (American Bank Note Co.) also bears this mark, the crispness and paper appearance here are consistent with the Continental #156 issue.*

For your consideration is a remarkable comparison pair of the 1c Blue Benjamin Franklin Bank Note issues.
