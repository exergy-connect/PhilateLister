### **Item Details**
*   **Scott Number:** Scott #11 (3¢ Dull Red, Type II, issued 1851-1857).
*   **Condition:** Used. This is an **imperforate** stamp (issued without perforations). It possesses four margins; the right, top, and bottom margins are clear, while the left margin is cut close to the outer frame line. The color is a rich dull red characteristic of this issue. No obvious thins, creases, or tears are visible from the face of the stamp.
*   **Perforations:** None (Imperforate).
*   **Cancellation:** Features a bold, black circular date stamp (CDS). The date "**22 SEP**" is clearly struck across the center. The town name at the top is partially visible, starting with "**D**" and possibly including a "**W**," which could suggest a town such as **DeWitt** (New York or Iowa) or **Delaware**. 
    *   *Note on Date:* Depending on the year of use (between 1851 and 1857), September 22nd fell on a Monday (1851/1856), Wednesday (1852), Thursday (1853), Friday (1854), Saturday (1855), or Tuesday (1857).

### **Description**
For your consideration is a fine example of the **1851-1857 3-cent George Washington (Scott #11)** in the "dull red" shade. This imperforate classic is an essential piece for any collector of early United States philately.

This particular specimen is notable for its very clear and bold **September 22nd** town cancellation. The stamp displays four margins, with a particularly tight margin on the left side that just touches the frame line. The design remains sharp, and the rich color typical of the #11 issue is well-preserved.

The town name, while only partially legible, appears to be an upstate New York or midwestern location starting with "D". If the cancel is from **DeWitt, New York**, it would represent a community that was a growing agricultural and transit hub in Onondaga County during the mid-1850s, with a population of approximately 3,300 at the time.

A solid, attractive used classic for your album. Please see the high-resolution scan for a detailed look at the margins and cancellation.
