### **eBay Listing Title**
**Sweden 1874 Official 12ö Blue Scott O18 - Superb ÅSBO Nov 12, 1878 Bold Cancel**

### **Catalog Number**
**Scott #O18** (Sweden 12 öre blue, Small Coat of Arms official issue). This issue was first released in 1874 for government service mail.

### **Condition Report**
*   **Overall:** Used, with a very attractive bold cancellation.
*   **Centering:** Poorly centered; the design is shifted significantly to the left, encroaching on the perforations.
*   **Perforations:** The stamp has a standard perforation 13. There are approximately 14 perforation dents along the top and bottom edges and 17 along the sides. There is some minor wear and a few short teeth on the left edge.
*   **Paper:** Shows some light age-related toning, particularly visible on the left margin, but the blue ink remains vibrant.

### **Cancel Details**
*   **Location:** **ÅSBO**, a historical district (*härad*) in the Scania (Skåne) region of southern Sweden.
*   **Date:** November 12, 1878.
*   **Date Parsing:** Following the Swedish postal format of the era, the central number "12" indicates the day of the month. The bottom line "18 11 78" splits the year around the month: 18(78) with "11" (November) in the center.
*   **Verification:** November 12, 1878, fell on a **Tuesday**, which is a standard business day for postal operations, confirming the reading.

### **Regional Context**
In 1878, **Åsbo** was an important agricultural and administrative district in Scania. The region was a central hub for Sweden's grain production and was undergoing significant modernization due to the expansion of the Swedish railway network. The population of the combined North and South Åsbo districts at the time was approximately 45,000 people, spread across various rural parishes and small trading towns.
