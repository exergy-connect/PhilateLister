This is a classic Swedish official stamp, identified as **Sweden Scott #O5**, the 12 öre blue "Tjenestefrimärke" (Service Stamp) from the 1874 issue. 

### **eBay Listing Details**

**Title:** Sweden Official 1874 12ö Blue Scott #O5 - Clear SÄFSJÖ CDS Nov 18, 1878 (Monday)

**Catalog Number:** 
*   **Scott Number:** **O5** (Perf 14). The perforation count across the top (approx. 14 teeth) identifies this as the 1874 issue rather than the later Perf 13 variety (#O16).

**Condition Report:**
*   **Overall:** Used. The stamp displays some light overall toning and surface soiling consistent with its age.
*   **Perforations:** The stamp is perforated 14. There are approximately 14 horizontal teeth and 17 vertical teeth. Notably, there is **one missing perforation tooth** at the upper-left corner.
*   **Cancel:** It features a very clear and well-centered black circular date stamp (CDS).

**Cancellation Details:**
*   **Town:** **SÄFSJÖ** (the 19th-century spelling of modern-day **Sävsjö**).
*   **Date:** **November 18, 1878**. Based on the calendar for that year, this date fell on a **Monday**.
*   **Historical Context:** In 1878, Säfsjö was a burgeoning community in the province of Småland. It grew rapidly following the arrival of the Southern Main Line (*Södra stambanan*) in 1864. By the time this stamp was cancelled, the town had become a vital railway station junction, serving as a hub for both regional trade and the expanding Swedish industrial network.
