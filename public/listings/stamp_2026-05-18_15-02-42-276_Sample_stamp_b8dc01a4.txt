This 1874 Sweden 12 öre blue official stamp (Tjänstefrimärke) is a classic example of Swedish postal history, featuring a remarkably clear and well-centered "ÅSBO" circular date stamp.

### **eBay Listing Title**
Sweden 12ö Blue Official Stamp Scott O6 Used - Clear ÅSBO 12 Nov 1876 CDS

### **Catalog Identification**
*   **Catalog Number:** Scott #O6 (issued 1874). 
*   **Design:** Coat of Arms of Sweden with supporting lions and crown, inscribed "SVERIGE" and "TJENSTEFRIMÄRKE."
*   **Color:** Blue.

### **Condition Report**
*   **Overall Grade:** Fine-Very Fine (F-VF) for the issue.
*   **Centering:** Well-centered with the design clear of the perforations on all sides.
*   **Margins:** Notably wide margin on the left side, potentially a sheet-edge margin, showing original paper texture. There is some light atmospheric toning/soiling consistent with its age, particularly visible on the left margin.
*   **Perforations:** The stamp is perforated 13. All perforations are present; there are approximately 14 horizontal and 17 vertical perforation tips. The teeth are generally clean, with some minor irregularity typical of 19th-century Swedish line-perforated issues.

### **Cancel Details**
*   **Location:** Åsbo, Sweden. 
*   **Date:** 12 Nov 1876 (12/11/76).
*   **Historical Note:** November 12, 1876, was a **Sunday**. While many smaller post offices had restricted hours, the presence of a Sunday cancel adds a layer of postal history interest to this piece.
*   **Region:** Åsbo is a rural parish in Östergötland. During the late 19th century, it was a traditional agricultural community. The population of the parish at the time of this cancel was approximately 2,000 residents. The local post office served as a vital communication link for the farming region of southern Sweden.

### **Item Description**
Offered here is a very attractive used example of the Swedish 12 öre Blue Official stamp, Scott #O6. This stamp is distinguished by a bold, black ÅSBO circular date stamp dated November 12, 1876. 

The stamp features strong, vibrant color and excellent eye appeal. The clear cancel does not overly obscure the central coat of arms design. As noted in the dealer's records, the 1876 date is well within the period of use for this issue (released in 1874). This is a great addition for collectors of Swedish officials or those specializing in Östergötland postal history.
