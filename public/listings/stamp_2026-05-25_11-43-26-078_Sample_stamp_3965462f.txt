This 1878 Swedish Official stamp is a characteristic example of the period's postal history, featuring a very clear and legible town cancellation. While the stamp shows significant physical wear, it remains a useful reference for its distinct cancellation from the Åsbo region.

### Identification & Catalog Number
*   **Country:** Sweden (Sverige)
*   **Type:** Official Stamp (Tjenstefrimärke)
*   **Scott Number:** **O7** (12 öre Blue, issued circa 1874-1884)
*   **Issue Series:** Large Coat of Arms Official Series

### Condition Report
*   **Overall Grade:** Faulty / Space-Filler.
*   **Physical Damage:** There is a significant vertical tear and a large missing section along the entire left edge of the stamp.
*   **Surface:** The center of the stamp remains bright, though the heavy black cancel obscures some of the fine engraving details.
*   **Perforations:** The stamp originally featured a perforation of 13. Currently, there are approximately 13 horizontal perforation teeth across the top and bottom. On the right vertical edge, there are 14 teeth. The left edge is severely compromised with most teeth missing due to the tear.

### Cancellation Details
*   **Town:** **ÅSBO** (Located in the Östergötland region). At the time of this cancellation, Åsbo was a primarily agricultural parish with a modest population concentrated in small farming communities.
*   **Date:** **18 1 78** (January 18, 1878). 
*   **Verification:** January 18, 1878, fell on a **Friday**. This is a standard postal operating day, confirming the cancel is a genuine contemporary strike.
*   **Numbers in the Cancel:** Per the dealer's specific request, the visible numbers in the cancel are **18**, **1**, and **78** (representing the day, month, and year). Note that the number **12** (the denomination) appears three times in the stamp design itself, but is not part of the postal cancel strike.
