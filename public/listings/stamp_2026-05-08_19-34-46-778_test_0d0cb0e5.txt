This is a classic **Denmark 20 Øre Bicolor issue**, featuring a distinct three-ring numeral cancel.

### Identification
*   **Country:** Denmark
*   **Issue:** Bicolor "Numeral" series
*   **Denomination:** 20 Øre
*   **Catalog Number:** Likely **Scott #20** (issued 1875, perf 14 x 13.5). While later printings exist (e.g., Scott #45 or #52), the use of a numeral cancel strongly suggests an earlier printing, as these cancels were officially discontinued in 1884.
*   **Color:** Rose (frame) and Gray/Slate (center).

### Cancel Analysis
*   **Numeral:** **152**
*   **Cancel Type:** Three-ring numeral.
*   **Town Assignment:** **Skibby** (located on the island of Sjælland).
*   **Historical Context:** Postal numeral 152 was originally assigned to Flensborg. After the Second Schleswig War in 1864, Flensborg was ceded to Prussia/Austria, and the "152" numeral was reassigned to Skibby in October 1864. This stamp represents the Skibby period of use.
*   **Consistency Check:** The 20 Øre denomination was introduced in 1875. The "152" cancel remained in use at Skibby until numeral cancels were withdrawn in late 1884, providing a clear usage window for this stamp.

### Condition Report
*   **Centering:** Off-center toward the bottom right.
*   **Perforations:** Generally sound and complete for the issue, with no major visible faults or missing teeth.
*   **Cancel Quality:** Heavy but very legible; the "152" digits are clearly identifiable in the center of the rings.
*   **Paper/Color:** Colors remain vibrant; no obvious thinning or tears are visible from the face.
