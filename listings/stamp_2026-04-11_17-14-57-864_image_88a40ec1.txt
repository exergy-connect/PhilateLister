Scott 38 [1877-1886 Issue, 1kr bister & blue, Perforation 14]

Used, possessing Very Fine centering for this classically difficult high value. The stamp bears a remarkably heavy, over-inked town circular date stamp that covers much of the bister frame and touches the central blue medallion. While the basic design and the central Three Crowns remain legible, the sheer density of the postmark dominates the visual presentation. Close inspection reveals generally sound perforations, though a few tips—particularly along the top edge—are slightly softened or blunted, typical of the period's separation and exacerbated slightly by the heavy cancel strike. The 1 Krona bister and blue represented the highest denomination of its immediate series, introduced as Sweden transitioned its currency, and remains a cornerstone of 19th-century Swedish philately. While premium examples are actively prized for crisp, light cancels that showcase the bi-color printing, this example serves as a boldly struck, correctly centered representative of the issue. Standard catalog valuations for Scott 38 (and its equivalent Facit 38) typically assume a lighter, more legible cancel, but the inherent difficulty of finding well-centered copies of this denomination maintains baseline specialist interest.
