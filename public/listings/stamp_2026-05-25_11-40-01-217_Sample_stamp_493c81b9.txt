*   **Perforations:** Perforated 14. The perforation count is approximately 14-15 holes horizontally and 18-20 vertically. The left edge shows a very wide, somewhat ragged margin, possibly indicating it was near a sheet edge or roughly separated. There are a few short perfs at the top left.
*   **Face/Paper:** There is some visible light foxing/soiling on the left-hand margin and top edge. The paper appears sound otherwise.

### **Cancel Information**
*   **City:** **ÅSBO**, Sweden.
*   **Date:** January 18, 1878 (**18 / 1 / 78**).
*   **Weekday:** This date was a **Friday**. The cancel is a crisp, legible circular date stamp (CDS) in black ink.
*   **Historical Context:** In 1878, Åsbo (located in Östergötland County) was a rural agricultural parish with a population of approximately 1,700–1,800 people. The post office there served a local farming community. Official stamps like this were strictly for government and administrative mail, making this a piece of 19th-century Swedish civil service history.
