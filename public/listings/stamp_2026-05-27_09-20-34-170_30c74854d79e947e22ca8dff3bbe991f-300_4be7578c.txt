### Stamp Identification
*   **Catalog Number:** Scott #19 (based on dealer notes and design/color matching).
*   **Issue Year:** 1872
*   **Denomination:** 1 Cent
*   **Design:** Dutch Coat of Arms (crowned lion with sword and arrows) within a wreath.

### Condition Report
*   **General Appearance:** A very clean used example with a well-placed circular date stamp (CDS).
*   **Centering:** Good centering for this 19th-century issue, slightly shifted towards the upper-left corner.
*   **Perforations:** Complete set of perforations. The horizontal count is approximately 14 tips, and the vertical count is approximately 18 tips, which is consistent with the standard perforation 14 for this series. All teeth appear intact without damage.
*   **Cancel Details:** A clear, partial strike of the circular date stamp for **'s-Gravenhage** (The Hague). 
    *   **Date:** 13 AUG (August 13th).
    *   **Time Indicator:** The bottom of the cancel reads "12-1 V," indicating a time block of 12:00 to 1:00 PM (*Voormiddag* indicates the period including noon). 
    *   **Year:** The year is not visible on this strike, but the stamp was in use from 1872 through the early 1890s.

### Historical Context
**'s-Gravenhage**, commonly known as **The Hague**, is the administrative and royal capital of the Netherlands. At the time this stamp was likely cancelled (circa 1880), the city had a population of approximately 113,000 and was expanding rapidly as a major political and diplomatic hub for the region.
