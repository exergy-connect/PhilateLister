This pair of 1-cent blue Benjamin Franklin stamps includes two distinct issues from the 1870s, identifiable by the presence (or absence) of a "secret mark."

### eBay Listing Title
**US Scott 145 & 156 1c Blue Benjamin Franklin - National & Continental Bank Note Co. Pair**

### Catalog Identification
*   **Left Stamp: Scott #156 (1873).** This is the Continental Bank Note Company printing. It is distinguished by the **secret mark**.
*   **Right Stamp: Scott #145 (1870).** This is the National Bank Note Company printing. It lacks the secret mark.

### The Secret Mark Description
The dealer asked to identify and describe the secret mark. For the 1-cent denomination:
*   **Location:** Look at the bottom of the design, between the words "ONE" and "CENT." There is a numeral "1" flanked by two white circular balls (ornaments).
*   **The Mark:** On the **left stamp (Scott 156)**, there is a small, distinct, dark **crescent-shaped line** inside the white ball to the left of the numeral "1."
*   **Comparison:** On the **right stamp (Scott 145)**, this ball is clear and white, without the added crescent mark.

### Condition Report
*   **Left Stamp (#156):** Centered slightly to the left. The color is deep and vibrant. Perforations (gauge 12) appear mostly intact, with about 11-12 horizontal "dents" and 14-15 vertical "dents" per side. No visible cancel; appears to be unused.
*   **Right Stamp (#145):** Centered to the right. The color is slightly more muted/faded compared to the left example. Perforations are gauge 12; some "short" perfs are visible along the top edge. No visible cancel; appears to be unused.
*   **Cancels:** Neither stamp displays a visible postal cancellation or town mark.

### Listing Body Text
Presented here is an excellent study pair of the 1-cent Benjamin Franklin issues of the 1870s. This pair highlights the transition from the National Bank Note Company to the Continental Bank Note Company. 

The stamp on the left is **Scott #156**, featuring the "secret mark" added by Continental: a small crescent inside the ball to the left of the bottom numeral "1." The stamp on the right is **Scott #145**, the original National printing which lacks this mark. Both stamps are perf 12 and appear to be in unused condition with no visible cancels. The left stamp shows particularly strong color and fresh eye appeal, while the right stamp provides a perfect reference for the unmarked original design. A must-have for any specialist of the Bank Note era.
