**Title**
Netherlands Scott #41 50c Bistre Coat of Arms - Used 's-Hertogenbosch 1883 CDS

**Key Facts**
* **Country:** Netherlands
* **Issue:** 1876-1894 Coat of Arms Series
* **Scott Number:** #41
* **Denomination:** 50 Cent
* **Color:** Bistre
* **Cancel:** Clear 's-Hertogenbosch circular date stamp (CDS), dated 8 OCT 83 (1883).

**Condition**
A sound used example with attractive centering for the issue and a very legible provincial capital cancel.

**Description**
This is a sharp, used example of the Netherlands 1876 50-cent bistre from the long-running Coat of Arms series. This high-denomination value features the Dutch lion within a circular frame, topped by the royal crown. The stamp is notable for its exceptional cancel clarity; it bears a complete 's-Hertogenbosch circular date stamp dated October 8, 1883. 's-Hertogenbosch, the capital of North Brabant, is a favorite among collectors of Dutch town cancels. The bistre color remains strong, providing a fine contrast against the black postal marking. The centering is quite good for this period, with the design well-contained within the perforations. A great addition for a specialist in Dutch postal history or the "Small Numerals" era.

**Condition Summary**
Used, sound, with a clear 's-Hertogenbosch 1883 circular date stamp.
