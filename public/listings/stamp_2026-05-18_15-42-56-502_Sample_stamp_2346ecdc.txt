### **Condition Report**
This used stamp is in fair to good condition. The centering is shifted towards the bottom right. There is significant toning or soiling visible on the left side of the stamp, particularly in the wide margin. The left edge shows signs of wear, with several blunt or shortened perforations.

### **Perforations**
*   **Type:** Perf 14.
*   **Count:** Approximately 18 horizontal perforations and 13 vertical perforations.
*   **Damage:** The left side exhibits irregular perforation tips, with some appearing clipped or worn down.

### **Cancel Details**
*   **City:** **ÅSBO**
*   **Date:** November 12, 1876 (as per dealer notes and visible "12 11 76")
*   **Weekday Check:** November 12, 1876, fell on a **Sunday**. 
*   **Note:** Sunday cancellations are historically unusual for Swedish post offices of this era. It is possible the date digit is a partial strike of another number (such as "13" or "18") or represents a rare instance of Sunday postal processing at a small rural station.

### **City Information**
Åsbo is a rural parish located in the province of Östergötland. At the time of this cancellation in 1876, the parish had a population of approximately **1,675 inhabitants**. The Åsbo post station had only recently been established on January 1, 1875, making this a relatively early and desirable strike from a small-town office.

### **Listing Body**
Offered here is a Swedish Official stamp (Tjenstefrimärke), Scott #O6, issued in 1874. This 12 öre blue stamp features a remarkably bold and readable circular date stamp from the small town of **ÅSBO**.

As noted by the dealer, the cancel is dated November 12, 1876. Interestingly, this date was a Sunday, which may pique the interest of postal history specialists looking for unusual date strikes or small-town "landstorm" postal artifacts. While the stamp shows some typical aging and minor perforation wear on the left side, the central coat of arms design remains crisp, and the "ÅSBO" town name is exceptionally clear. This is an excellent addition for collectors of Swedish officials or provincial cancellations.
