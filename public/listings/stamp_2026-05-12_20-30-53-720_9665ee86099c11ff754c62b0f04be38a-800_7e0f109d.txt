This is a United States 1-cent Benjamin Franklin stamp, part of the "Bank Note" issues of the 1870s.

### **eBay Listing Title**
**USA 1873 1c Benjamin Franklin Blue (Scott 156) Unused - w/ Secret Mark**

### **Catalog Identification**
*   **Scott Number:** **#156** (Continental Bank Note Company, 1873). It could also be **#182** (American Bank Note Company, 1879) if the paper is soft and porous. Given the crispness of the impression, it is likely the Continental printing.
*   **Justification:** The presence of the **"secret mark"** identifies this as a Continental Bank Note Company issue or later. The original 1870 issue by National (#145) lacks this mark.

### **Condition Report**
*   **General:** An unused example with very fresh, bright blue color and a sharp impression.
*   **Centering:** Off-center towards the top and right, which is common for this issue.
*   **Perforations:** Perf 12. Most perforation teeth are intact and full; there is some minor blunting/shortening on the upper right edge.
    *   **Horizontal Count:** 12 teeth across the top and bottom.
    *   **Vertical Count:** 15 teeth along the left and right sides.
*   **Cancel:** None. The stamp appears in mint/unused condition (gum status cannot be determined from the front).

### **Secret Mark Description**
As you noted, this issue is famous for its "secret marks." 
*   **Location:** The secret mark for the 1-cent Franklin is located in the small circular ball ornament situated immediately to the left of the numeral **"1"** at the bottom center of the design.
*   **Description:** Inside this small ball, there is a distinct, small crescent-shaped dash of blue ink. In the 1870 National Bank Note issue, this ball is completely clear or has only uniform shading. Its presence here confirms this as a Continental or American Bank Note Co. printing.

### **Listing Body**
This is a striking example of the 1873 1-cent Benjamin Franklin in blue. This specific stamp is highly desirable for collectors because it clearly displays the **"secret mark"** used to identify the work of the Continental Bank Note Company.

The secret mark can be found in the small pearl/ball ornament just to the left of the central "1" at the bottom of the stamp; it appears as a small, curved crescent mark within the circle. This diagnostic feature distinguishes this printing from the earlier 1870 National Bank Note Co. issue.

The stamp is in unused condition with no visible cancellation. It boasts deep, rich color and excellent eye appeal. While centered toward the top-right, the overall freshness of the piece makes it a wonderful representative of this classic 19th-century American issue.
